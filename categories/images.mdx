Image OSINT is a multi-layered discipline. At its most basic, reverse image search lets you discover where an image appears online — useful for debunking impersonation, detecting stolen profile photos, or tracing the origin of a suspicious picture. Going deeper, facial recognition tools can match an unknown face against indexed public photos to identify a subject. EXIF metadata extraction pulls hidden data baked into image files — GPS coordinates, camera model, software used, and timestamp — which can geolocate a photo or reveal when and how it was captured. Image forensics tools detect manipulation and inconsistencies in pixel-level data, helping verify whether a photo has been altered.

<Warning>
  Facial recognition tools raise significant legal and ethical concerns. Use of these services to identify individuals without their consent may be unlawful under GDPR, the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA), and other regional privacy frameworks. Many platforms also prohibit using facial recognition against their users. Always confirm the legality of facial recognition use within your jurisdiction and engagement scope before proceeding.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **FotoForensics** and **Jimpl** are excellent for extracting EXIF metadata from images — useful for geolocating photos or verifying authenticity. When a subject shares an image, always check its metadata before assuming the location or timestamp is accurate, as metadata can be stripped or falsified.
</Tip>
