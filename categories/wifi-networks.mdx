---

Wireless network intelligence plays a valuable role in both offensive and defensive security operations. Wardriving databases aggregate access point locations contributed by users worldwide, enabling researchers to perform access point geolocation, map network density in target areas, and assist in physical security assessments. These datasets can be cross-referenced with other OSINT sources to verify physical locations, track infrastructure changes over time, and understand the wireless footprint of a target environment.

<Note>
  Wigle.net is the most comprehensive public wardriving database with over 1 billion networks. Data is user-contributed from wardriving surveys around the world.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Passively querying WiFi databases is legal. Actively connecting to networks without authorization is not.
</Warning>
