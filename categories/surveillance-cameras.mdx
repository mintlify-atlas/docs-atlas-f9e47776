---
title: "Public Webcam Directories & Surveillance Camera Search"
sidebarTitle: "Surveillance Cameras"
description: "Directories of publicly accessible webcams, traffic cameras, and surveillance feeds for OSINT geolocation and physical security research."
---

Publicly accessible webcams and surveillance feeds are a valuable — and often overlooked — source of open source intelligence. Security researchers use public camera directories to understand physical environments, verify the accuracy of claimed locations, observe target facilities from a distance, and study the surveillance infrastructure deployed in specific areas. Traffic cameras, city webcams, and border crossing feeds provide real-time geographic context that complements other OSINT sources. Understanding where cameras exist and what they can see is also a fundamental part of physical security assessments and red team planning.

<Note>
  Tools like Insecam.org index cameras that are accidentally exposed due to default credentials or misconfigured network settings. Legitimate security use includes identifying and securing your own organization's cameras before an attacker does.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Only access cameras you own or have explicit permission to view. Unauthorized access to private surveillance systems is illegal in most jurisdictions.
</Warning>
