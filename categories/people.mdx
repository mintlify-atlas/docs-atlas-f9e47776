People OSINT draws from an unusually diverse set of sources: public records (voter registrations, court filings, property records), aggregated commercial data (people-search sites), social media profiles, username enumeration across platforms, and historical genealogy databases. Effective people investigations typically combine multiple approaches — starting with a name or email in a people-search engine, pivoting to username tools to discover linked accounts, and using genealogy resources to fill in historical context. Because data quality and coverage vary by country and database, cross-referencing across several services is essential for accuracy.

<Note>
  Data accuracy varies significantly by region. People-search databases in the US tend to be the most comprehensive due to open public records laws, while coverage of individuals in Europe, Asia, and other regions may be limited, outdated, or unavailable due to stricter data protection regulations like GDPR. When using these tools in professional or legal contexts, always verify findings through primary sources. Public records searches can surface sensitive personal information — handle results responsibly and in accordance with applicable privacy laws.
</Note>
